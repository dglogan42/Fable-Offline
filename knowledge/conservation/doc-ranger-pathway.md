## What DOC rangers do (post framing)

Crucial **mahi** looking after:

- Native species  
- Natural environment  
- Public facilities (tracks, huts, visitor assets)  

Context named: aim to be **Predator Free by 2050** — younger generations called out as having a big role.

---

## Qualification path (as of 2020 blog)

1. Complete **New Zealand Certificate in Conservation Operations, Level 4**  
2. Providers cited:  
   - [NMIT — Kaitiaki Whenua / Trainee Ranger](https://www.nmit.ac.nz/study/programmes/kaitiaki-whenua-trainee-ranger) (designed with DOC support for trainee/ranger-ready skills)  
   - [Toi Ohomai — NZ Certificate in Conservation Operations L4](https://toiohomai.ac.nz/study/new-zealand-certificate-conservation-operations-level-4)  
3. Graduates may **apply** for a **limited number** of Trainee Ranger vacancies at DOC each year (not a guaranteed job)  

Careers.govt.nz also lists [ranger](https://www.careers.govt.nz/jobs-database/animal-care-and-conservation/conservation/ranger/) and [conservation industry](https://www.careers.govt.nz/jobs-database/animal-care-and-conservation/#industry-conservation) pages — VERIFY LIVE.
