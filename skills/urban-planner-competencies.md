# Urban planner competencies

**WHEN_TO_USE:** Career or study questions about urban planning / urban design; skill audits for planners; learning plans; portfolio advice; linking postgraduate webinars (e.g. Master of Urban Design) to practice competencies; **freight / goods-movement / strategic freight network** work; multimodal transport plans (e.g. Auckland Transport Future Connect); **climate plans / emissions pathways / climate modeling** (e.g. Te Tāruke-ā-Tāwhiri: Auckland’s Climate Plan); or designing an agent that assists planning work (GIS notes, policy maps, stakeholder plans) without replacing licensed professionals.

## Stance
Urban planning blends **spatial analysis**, **policy knowledge**, and **stakeholder management** to design and implement community land-use strategies. **Freight planning** is a core multimodal strand: moving goods safely and efficiently while managing community, equity, and environmental effects. **Climate literacy** (mitigation pathways + adaptation) links land use, transport, and equity under uncertainty. Technical tools matter; so do negotiation, empathy, and creative problem-solving. This skill **coaches competence frameworks** — it does not practice law, certify planners, or approve developments.

**Not legal, planning, engineering, or climate-science advice.** Zoning, designation, and consent decisions require local professionals and statutory processes. Do not invent corridor classifications, freight hierarchy labels, or MtCO₂e pathway figures not in user-supplied sources.

## Four competence areas

### 1. Technical & analytical
| Competency | What good looks like |
|------------|----------------------|
| **GIS** | Map infrastructure, analyse land use, visualise spatial layers; cite CRS/data sources |
| **Data analysis & statistics** | Interpret demographic, economic, and environmental data; forecast needs with uncertainty labels |
| **CAD & 3D modelling** | Produce spatial plans / layouts others can critique; version assumptions |
| **Regulatory knowledge** | Local zoning, building codes, environmental rules, resource management — **jurisdiction-specific**; never invent bylaws |
| **Freight & goods data** | HGV/truck volumes, freight generators (ports, industrial, logistics parks), first/last-mile constraints; separate **observed** vs **modelled** |
| **Climate & emissions literacy** | Read GHG inventories and **pathway models** (baseline year, BAU vs action, residual); hazard/adaptation inputs — use skill `climate-modeling` |

### 2. Communication & interpersonal
| Competency | What good looks like |
|------------|----------------------|
| **Stakeholder engagement** | Involve residents, business, iwi/mana whenua (where relevant), environment groups with clear methods |
| **Freight stakeholders** | Port/airport operators, logistics firms, industrial landowners, road controlling authorities, rail operators, local communities on freight routes |
| **Public speaking & presentation** | Translate technical work for boards and the public without hiding trade-offs |
| **Mediation & negotiation** | Facilitate when developers, policymakers, and residents conflict — including **freight amenity vs economic access** |
| **Team collaboration** | Work with architects, civil engineers, economists, transport / freight planners |

### 3. Design & strategic
| Competency | What good looks like |
|------------|----------------------|
| **Creative vision** | See alternative social/physical futures for a place; test against constraints |
| **Master planning & revitalisation** | Shape public space, density, open space, sustainable transit systems |
| **Multimodal strategic networks** | Integrate passenger modes **and freight**; avoid “people-only” plans that ignore goods movement |
| **Freight network planning** | Hierarchy (strategic / supporting), mode split (road, rail, coastal, air), land-use fit (industrial zoning near corridors), time-of-day / access management |
| **Project evaluation** | Feasibility, sustainability, equity/social impact — with criteria and evidence (include freight reliability, severance, noise, emissions where relevant) |
| **Climate-responsive planning** | Align growth/transport options with mitigation + adaptation goals; avoid treating illustrative pathways as project carbon certificates |

### 4. Management & organisation
| Competency | What good looks like |
|------------|----------------------|
| **Decision making** | Weigh options under budget and land constraints; document why |
| **Leadership & organisation** | Multi-project delivery; staff oversight; alignment with mandates and plans |
| **Programme alignment** | Tie local freight actions to regional transport plans, port strategies, RLTP/LTP-style investment pathways (names vary by place); align with **regional climate plans** where they exist |

## Freight plan module (core)

Freight planning asks: **How do goods move now and in the first decade ahead; where is the network strategic; where are deficiencies, conflicts, and opportunities; what land-use and network responses are defensible?**

### Freight plan building blocks
| Block | Questions |
|-------|-----------|
| **Demand & generators** | What produces/attracts freight (port, warehouse, construction, retail distribution)? |
| **Network hierarchy** | Which links are **strategic freight** vs supporting? (e.g. Future Connect “Freight” mode) |
| **Mode** | Road heavy vehicle, rail freight, coastal shipping, air cargo — what is in scope? |
| **Land use** | Are industrial/logistics zones served? Conflicts with sensitive uses? |
| **First/last mile** | Kerbside loading, HPMV routes, bridge/height limits, local street amenity |
| **Time period** | Current vs future decade (align with plan horizons) |
| **Problems** | Safety, congestion, emissions/environment, equity (who bears truck impacts?) |
| **Opportunities** | Mode shift to rail/coastal, consolidation centres, protected corridors, quiet deliveries |
| **Intermodal** | Transfer points (port-rail, rail-road); do passenger priorities block freight? |
| **Evidence** | Counts, models, crash data, community complaints — source and date |

### Freight evaluation criteria (default set)
Use explicitly; score or narrative each:
1. **Economic access** — reliability for freight generators  
2. **Safety** — crash risk, conflicts with active modes  
3. **Amenity & equity** — noise, severance, exposure of disadvantaged communities  
4. **Environment** — emissions, sensitive environments  
5. **Network resilience** — alternate routes, pinch points  
6. **Land-use compatibility** — zoning and future growth  
7. **Deliverability** — cost, designation, construction, operations  

### Auckland Future Connect (worked practice surface)
When using AT **Future Connect** mapping portal (`knowledge/urban-planning/at-future-connect-portal.md`):
1. Output: Strategic Network **or** Transport System Analysis **or** Focus Areas  
2. Mode: **Freight** (also compare Integrated Network)  
3. Period: **Current** vs **First Decade**  
4. Optional: supporting networks, contextual layers  
5. TSA intermodal: Environment / Safety / Equity when available  
6. Cross-check downloadable reports at https://at.govt.nz/FutureConnect via `pdf-render`  
7. Privacy of the portal itself: `knowledge/privacy/at-future-connect-hosts.md`  

**Do not invent** Future Connect layer attributes not observed in the portal or official PDFs.

## audit-competencies

Score each row **1–5** (1 = no evidence, 5 = can teach others) with **evidence** (project, course, work sample) or **gap**.

Include freight rows when the user works in transport/infrastructure:
- Freight & goods data  
- Freight stakeholders  
- Freight network planning  
- Multimodal strategic networks  

Output:
1. Verdict first — strength cluster + biggest gap cluster  
2. Score table (four areas, all rows; freight rows if in scope)  
3. Top 3 gaps with one concrete practice task each  
4. What not to claim (e.g. “I know all zoning” without jurisdiction; “strategic freight route X” without source)

## plan-growth

**Input:** current level, target role (council, consultancy, NGO, research, **transport/freight**), time horizon.

**Steps:**
1. Pick **one** technical skill and **one** interpersonal skill per quarter (don’t boil ocean).  
2. Map to real artifacts: map package, policy memo, engagement plan, 3D option set, **freight issues paper**, **mode comparison map**.  
3. Prefer local statutory literacy (district plan / RMA or local equivalent) over generic “urban theory only.”  
4. For freight track: one artifact that pairs **network map + generator map + conflict note**.  
5. End with **this month’s build** (one deliverable).  

Align with agentic habits: goal → do → verify with a critic (peer/mentor) → stop or iterate (`loop-engineer`).

## map-programme

When evaluating a course, webinar, or degree (e.g. Master of Urban Design / transport planning):

1. Map claimed learning outcomes → the four competence areas **+ freight/multimodal if claimed**.  
2. Flag marketing vs evidence (use `education-claim-audit` if accreditation claims appear).  
3. List what the programme **won’t** replace (years of statutory practice, GIS portfolio, freight operations literacy).  
4. Next verification: faculty, studio load, software stack, studio city/context.  

Cross-link: `knowledge/privacy/uoa-eloqua-pg-webinar-hosts.md` if the surface is a UoA marketing LP (privacy ≠ curriculum quality).

## design-planning-agent

Design an agent that **assists** planners (not an autonomous consent authority).

| Component | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Goal | Analyse, summarise, draft options, structure engagement, **structure freight option sets** — **HITL on recommendations** |
| Tools | Read local plan excerpts user provides; GIS **data** user supplies; write memos under workspace; PDF extracts via `pdf-render` |
| Forbidden | Inventing zone rules; fabricating freight volumes or hazard layers; submitting statutory applications |
| Verifier | Re-derive numbers; label guesses; cite user-supplied sources only |
| Privacy | Stakeholder contact lists and submissions = PII → use `privacy-design-planner` |

Stack with Fable: `/team` for research→write→critic on policy memos; `/engineer` for scored option evaluation; `/privacy` if web tooling is involved; `--pdf` for AT/strategy PDFs.

## plan-engagement

Structure a stakeholder process:
1. Purpose and decision rights (who decides what)  
2. Who is affected (map interests, power, information needs) — include **freight operators** and **corridor communities** when goods movement is in scope  
3. Methods (workshop, survey, drop-in, hui) + accessibility  
4. What is negotiable vs fixed (budget, hazard, legal, port operating requirements)  
5. Feedback loop (how input changes the plan)  
6. Risk: tokenism — require at least one “what changed because of engagement” field  

---

## plan-freight

**Input:** study area, horizon (current / first decade / other), modes in scope, any maps/PDFs/extracts user supplies.

**Process:**
1. **Scope** — geographic extent, modes, time horizon, decision the plan informs.  
2. **Evidence pack** — generators, counts/models, crash/amenity issues, existing strategic freight layers (cite sources).  
3. **Network picture** — strategic vs supporting; pinch points; intermodal nodes.  
4. **Conflicts** — active modes, schools/sensitive uses, town centres, equity exposure.  
5. **Options** — network, land use, operations (hours, routes), mode shift; at least one “do minimum.”  
6. **Evaluate** with the freight criteria table (economic access … deliverability).  
7. **Recommended package** — verdict first; uncertainties explicit.  
8. **Next statutory/investment steps** — what process owns the decision (without inventing legal status).  

**Output shape:**
1. Verdict first (priority problems + direction of travel)  
2. Scope & sources table  
3. Generators & network summary  
4. Issues / opportunities list  
5. Options vs criteria matrix  
6. Recommended package + risks  
7. Engagement needs  
8. Explicit non-claims  

Local knowledge: `knowledge/urban-planning/freight-plan.md`, Future Connect portal notes.

## map-open-space-capacity

When sports field / parks **capacity development** programmes are in scope (e.g. AC Sports field capacity development programme):

1. **Investment** — quantum, horizon, regional vs local board split.  
2. **Need statement** — shortage type (e.g. training), spatial equity.  
3. **Targets** — demand % and year; flag if target date is historical.  
4. **Interventions** — artificial turf, sand carpet, renew, light, drain, new fields.  
5. **Governance** — annual review, partners (clubs, RSOs).  
6. **Risks named by council** — geotech, contamination, demand shift, tech, land.  
7. Evaluation questions (demand model, capacity metric, equity by area).  
8. Privacy of project page if HTML provided.  

Local seed: `knowledge/urban-planning/ac-sports-field-capacity-programme.md`.  
Do not invent which parks are funded without a project list source.

## map-compliance-policy

When Auckland Council (or similar) **compliance / enforcement policy** pages are in scope:

1. State **purpose** (environment, health, safety, well-being).  
2. List **coverage topics** (laws used, principles, encourage vs respond, risk, complaints, cost recovery).  
3. Flag **status** (current / under review / renewal date claims).  
4. Separate **landing summary** from full policy PDF if only HTML summary is available.  
5. Cross-link privacy of the web page if HTML dump provided (`privacy-host-map`).  
6. Local seed: `knowledge/urban-planning/ac-compliance-policy.md`.  

**Do not** invent prosecution criteria or fee schedules.

## plan-climate

When climate plans or modelling enter an urban/transport brief:

1. Hand primary modelling audit to skill **`climate-modeling`** (`structure-plan`, `map-modeling`, `audit-pathway`).  
2. Crosswalk sectors to spatial tools: transport/freight, built form, coastal communities.  
3. Use local knowledge: `knowledge/climate/auckland-climate-plan.md` for Te Tāruke-ā-Tāwhiri.  
4. Extract PDFs with `--pdf` / `pdf-render`.  
5. Output: planning-relevant verdict (what the pathway means for land use/transport options) + non-claims.  

**Auckland seed claims (verify in PDF):** −50% GHG by 2030 and net zero 2050 (2016 baseline); **CURB** (World Bank/C40) + supplementary modelling for an **illustrative** pathway; residual emissions remain in 2050 curve.

## future-connect-freight

Guided checklist for AT Future Connect (when user can access the portal or paste screenshots/exports):

1. Open portal; complete T&Cs only if user chooses (see privacy map).  
2. Select **Strategic Network** → mode **Freight** → period **Current**; note what the UI labels as visible.  
3. Switch period to **First Decade**; list differences **only if observed**.  
4. Switch to **Transport System Analysis** + Freight; enable Environment / Safety / Equity if available; record deficiencies language from legend/popups.  
5. Compare **Integrated Network** vs Freight-only.  
6. Optional: Focus Areas for freight-relevant regional challenges.  
7. Write a one-page **freight issues note** (workspace or knowledge) with sources = portal + AT.govt.nz/FutureConnect PDFs.  
8. Hand off to **plan-freight** for structured evaluation.

## Output shape (default coaching)

1. **Verdict first** (fit / gap / next step)  
2. Competence area focus (which of the four; freight if relevant)  
3. Concrete practice task (≤ 2 weeks)  
4. Artifact to produce (file or map package name)  
5. Verify how (mentor, checklist, public criteria, portal observation)  
6. Explicit non-claims

## Forbidden
- Approving land use or inventing bylaws  
- Presenting as a licensed planner without credentials  
- Hiding equity or environmental trade-offs to “sell” a vision  
- Treating webinar registration marketing as proof of competence  
- Inventing Future Connect or national freight hierarchy classifications  
- Treating GA/analytics on a map portal as planning evidence
