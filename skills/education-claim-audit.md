# Education & credential claim audit

**WHEN_TO_USE:** Evaluating a school, “university,” online M.A./Ph.D., fast-track doctorate, health coaching or lifestyle medicine certification, tuition-credit funnel, or any page that markets **accredited degrees** — especially after pasting site HTML or a URL summary (e.g. Lifestyle Prescriptions® University). For **legitimate NZ university programme hubs** (e.g. UC Arts postgraduate), prefer skill **`uc-arts-postgraduate`** for pathway mapping; still use this skill if someone **over-claims** accreditation or guaranteed outcomes.

## Stance
Marketing pages are **unverified claims**, not ground truth. Apply Section 4 (re-derive) and treat impressive accreditation logos as **guilty of sales framing** until primary registers and recognition rules are checked. “Accredited” is not one thing — always ask **by whom, for what, and recognized where**.

## What to extract from a site
1. Legal entity name, HQ country/state, founder/leadership  
2. Programs: certificate, diploma, bachelor, master, PhD, “fast-track”  
3. **Who issues the credential** (the brand vs a partner university)  
4. Accreditors / approvals named (ASIC, DEAC, regional US, national ministry, professional boards)  
5. State or ministry **authorization to operate** (license ≠ academic accreditation)  
6. Professional board pathways (NBHWC, IBLM, ACLM, nursing, medicine, etc.)  
7. Funding urgency: scholarships, “$X million pool,” limited spots, instant approval  
8. Medical/practice disclaimers vs marketing that implies clinical authority  
9. What is **missing**: CHEA/ED directory status, recognition for visas/jobs, refund policy, full tuition  

## Accreditation type cheat-sheet (claim hygiene)
| Label often used | Often actually means | Common gap |
|------------------|----------------------|------------|
| “Accredited university” | Private international accreditor (e.g. ASIC) | Not US regional accreditation |
| “Licensed school” | State proprietary school authorization | Not the same as accreditation |
| “Degrees from partner U” | Validation / franchise / dual certificate | Diploma may name partner, not the brand school |
| “CHEA” / “CIQG member” | Accreditor’s membership in a network | Does **not** auto-approve the school in US |
| “Board certification prep” | Eligible to sit an exam *if* prerequisites met | Degree recognition still separate |
| “Pending approval” | Applied, not completed | Homepage must not oversell as done |

## Checklist (score honesty of disclosure 1–10, not “school quality”)
| Gate | Question |
|------|----------|
| Entity clarity | Which legal entity enrolls the student and which issues the diploma? |
| Accreditor identity | Named body + status (premier, provisional) + link to **official directory**? |
| Recognition geography | Recognized for employment / further study / immigration in **user’s country**? |
| Degree issuer | Partner validation vs direct award — wording honest on homepage? |
| Prerequisites honesty | Fast-track without bachelor/master disclosed with recognition risks? |
| Board pathway | Professional board lists this program **today**? Pending vs approved? |
| Cost completeness | Full tuition, fees, scholarship conditions, refunds — not only “up to $X off” |
| Clinical claims | Diagnosis/treatment promised vs educational habit coaching only? |
| Missing evidence | What cannot be verified from the page alone? |

## Verdict labels (required)
- **Insufficient evidence** — cannot verify accreditor, issuer, or recognition from material given  
- **Marketing only** — claims present, primary sources not checked  
- **Partially verified** — some claims match public directories / state lists  
- **Red flags** — pending sold as approved, issuer obscured, regional-accreditation implication without regional accreditor, contradictory board status, medical practice implied despite disclaimers  

## Forbidden
- Recommending enrollment as “safe” or “equivalent to top research university” from a homepage  
- Equating ASIC / private foreign partners with US regional accreditation without evidence  
- Inventing ministry approvals, CHEA institutional status, or board exam eligibility  
- Medical, legal, or career advice  

## Output shape
1. **Verdict first**  
2. Table of claims vs evidence status (stated / missing / needs primary check)  
3. Accreditation **type** map (operate license vs institutional accred vs program validation vs CE)  
4. Inconsistencies (e.g. IBLM on nav vs “pending” on accred page)  
5. What the user must verify next (specific primary URLs / registers)  
6. One concrete risk of trusting the page as-is
